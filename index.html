I designed a random font using a website called Metaflop. I don't know what to call it. I'll keep it simple, MyFont. That's the name. The font leans more towards the abstract. It blurs the line between readability and symbolism. Some of the letters are wonky-looking. They have the shapes of stars. It's a clever font. You can use it on billboards, flyers, album covers, and more. MyFont reminds me of the 1960s infomercials or the old cartoon show called The Jetsons. Overall, I believe this is a unique font you can use to obfuscate a message. The viewer must look deeper to find the hidden meaning.
